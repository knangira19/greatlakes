About Us.

The Network is over 20 members strong, working in Uganda, East Africa to build a just and enabling environment for communities to thrive. We are dedicated organizations, individuals, academics, and activists. We come from rural and urban areas, community-based organizations, academic institutions and more. We are both women and men from all walks of life. We are people who believe that communities can be transformed and that we have the power and responsibility to do it! We believe marginalization has a very strong component in the fabric of our communities which impedes transformation.

How we work.

We document various issues related to GBV and VAW and use them as case studies to prevent similar occurrences amongst our network members. Ultimately, resources have been pinpointed at the center of most of the conflicts. In strategy we look at how to empower the Women and girls to be able to attain resources and capacity to fend for themselves and families as a way of minimizing the incidents. The shared experiences and interests encourage the desired change in communities to eventually achieve desired outcomes. Economic empowerment of the communities through Women led initiatives has led to a reduction of GBV and given a new voice to the victims overcome societal beliefs. The network organizes conferences where key resource persons are invited to present to embers and use documented findings to create more on ground impact. Focus groups are also engaged to share experiences and more insights into complex GBV occurrences in community.

Network Initiatives

We address GBV through various initiatives at the Network. We work at the prevention stage and forestalling eventuality instead of addressing outcomes of GBV after occurrence. Thematic Action Groups (TAGs) give members the opportunity to take specific action around a particular issue or area related to VAW Within a Thematic Action Group, members explore new tools for seeking social justice, practice putting thoughts into action, and share strategies for specific aspects of violence prevention.

Social Underlying issues in rural communities

At the root of the social structure and hierarchy is gender imbalance and violence against women (VAW). Understanding Violence Against Women (VAW): Traditional African Power set up between Men and Women is heavily in favor of men and marginalized women in access to opportunities and empowerment The network creates a platform to raise the voice against strong held beliefs about who controls power in a family and community set up with a view to creating a social balance for enhancement of equal rights between the two genders. We come together to spearhead social transformation in respective communities so that we distort the power imbalance that degenerates into GBV.

How we work

The Network exists for and because of its members! Members shape the work and values of the Network. A decision to admit members is on free and voluntary basis to both individuals and groups immersed in the gender-based violence space. Membership is free and open to any organization, group or individual working on VAW Prevention
